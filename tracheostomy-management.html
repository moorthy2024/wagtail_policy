Tracheostomy Management

Applicable NDIS Standards

Outcome Each participant with a tracheostomy receives appropriate suctioning and management of their tracheostomy relevant and proportionate to their individual needs.

Indicators

  • Each participant is involved in the assessment and development of the plan for their tracheostomy suctioning and management. With their consent, the participantâ€™s health status is subject to regular and timely review by an appropriately qualified health practitioner. The plan identifies how risks, incidents and emergencies will be managed, including required actions and escalation to ensure participant wellbeing.
  • Appropriate policies and procedures are in place, including a training plan for workers, that relate to the support provided to each participant with a tracheostomy.
  • All workers have completed training, relating specifically to each participantâ€™s needs, managing any tracheostomy related incident and high intensity support skills descriptor for providing tracheostomy care (without ventilation) and supporting a person dependent on ventilation, delivered by an appropriately qualified health practitioner or person that meets the high intensity support skills descriptor for tracheostomy suctioning and management.

Background and Definitions

General Medical Information:

Tracheostomy management encompasses the care and maintenance of a tracheostomy, a surgically created opening in the neck leading directly to the trachea (windpipe). This procedure is often necessary for individuals who require long-term ventilation support, have obstructed upper airways, or need frequent suctioning of respiratory secretions. Proper tracheostomy management is crucial to prevent complications such as infection, blockage of the tracheostomy tube, and damage to the trachea. It involves routine cleaning, suctioning, changing of the tracheostomy tube, and monitoring for signs of complications.

Definitions:

Tracheostomy: A surgical procedure to create a direct airway through an opening in the neck into the trachea.

Tracheostomy Tube: A tube inserted through the tracheostomy stoma to maintain an open airway.

Stoma: The opening on the neck surface created during a tracheostomy.

Suctioning: The process of removing mucus and secretions from the trachea and lower airways through the tracheostomy tube to prevent blockage and maintain clear airways.

Decannulation: The process of removing a tracheostomy tube when it is no longer needed.

Cuff Pressure Management: Regulating the pressure of the inflatable cuff on some tracheostomy tubes to ensure it is effective in providing ventilation support and preventing aspiration, yet not so high as to cause tracheal damage.

Tracheostomy Care: Routine care and maintenance of the tracheostomy site and tube, including cleaning and changing dressings.

Humidification: The process of adding moisture to the air breathed in through the tracheostomy tube to prevent drying of the respiratory tract.

Passy-Muir Valve: A type of speaking valve that can be attached to a tracheostomy tube, allowing for speech and improved breathing.

Stoma Care: Care of the skin and tissues around the tracheostomy opening to prevent infection and irritation.

Tracheostomy Weaning: Gradually decreasing dependence on a tracheostomy, eventually leading to decannulation.

Emergency Tracheostomy Kit: A set of essential supplies kept on hand to address urgent issues such as tube dislodgement or blockage.

Respiratory Assessment: Regular evaluation of breathing, oxygenation, and the effectiveness of ventilation through the tracheostomy.

Aspiration Precautions: Measures taken to prevent the entry of liquids or other materials into the lungs through the tracheostomy.

Policy

Dedication to delivering specialized and respectful tracheostomy care, prioritizing the individual needs and comfort of each participant.

Adherence to evidence-based practices and current clinical guidelines to ensure the highest standard of tracheostomy management.

Procedures

Participant Consent and Other Participant Considerations:

Establish a robust and ongoing consent process, ensuring participants or their guardians understand the nature and necessity of tracheostomy care.

Engage in regular discussions to reaffirm consent, particularly after any changes in health status or care protocols.

Assessment, Support/Care Plan Development and Review:

Initiate comprehensive, multidisciplinary assessments focusing on respiratory health, tracheostomy site integrity, and potential risks.

Collaborate with respiratory therapists, ENT specialists, and nursing staff to develop an individualized tracheostomy care plan.

Schedule regular plan reviews to adapt to changing health needs, participant preferences, or advancements in tracheostomy care.

Staff Training and Requirements:

Implement a detailed training program on tracheostomy care, including anatomy and physiology, suctioning techniques, infection control, and communication with ventilator-dependent individuals.

Organize routine skill assessments and emergency response drills to ensure staff preparedness.

Encourage participation in external training programs and conferences for continuous learning.

Risk Management:

Develop an extensive risk management plan addressing common tracheostomy-related risks such as infection, tube dislodgment, and aspiration.

Introduce regular monitoring routines for early detection of potential complications and immediate intervention.

Reporting:

Establish a comprehensive documentation system for all tracheostomy-related care activities, including detailed logs of suctioning, cleaning routines, and any observations of concern.

Develop a systematic approach for incident reporting and management, ensuring prompt and effective responses to any adverse events.

Prohibited Practices:

Define and communicate a list of prohibited practices in tracheostomy care, such as the use of unauthorized equipment or deviation from prescribed care protocols.

Regularly monitor and evaluate staff adherence to these practices, with disciplinary actions for non-compliance.
