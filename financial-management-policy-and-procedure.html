Financial Management Policy and Procedure

Purpose and Scope

This policy and procedure sets out Wagtail Servicesâ€™ financial management arrangements. It applies to Wagtail Servicesâ€™ Leadership Team and meets relevant legislation, regulations and Standards as set out in Schedule 1, Legislative References.

Applicable NDIS Practice Standards

Risk Management

Outcome: Risks to Participants, workers and the provider are identified and managed.

Indicators:

  • Risks to the organisation, including risks to Participants, financial and work health and safety risks, and risks associated with provision of supports are identified, analysed, prioritised and treated.
  • Support delivery is linked to a risk management system which includes:
    • Incident Management;
    • Complaints Management;
    • Work Health and Safety;
    • Human Resource Management;
    • Financial Management;
    • Information Management; and
    • Governance.

Definitions

Assets: non-consumable items of tangible property (including fixtures) that have a service life greater than one year. Assets can include, but are not limited to:

  • equipment or aids to support Participants;
  • electronic equipment (such as computers);
  • furniture; and
  • motor vehicles.

Corruption: dishonest activity where a person engaged by a business acts in a way that is contrary to the interests of the business and abuses their position to achieve a personal gain or advantage for themselves or others. 5

Fraud: dishonest activity causing actual or potential financial loss to any person or business, including theft. Fraud also includes the deliberate falsification, concealment, destruction or use of falsified documentation or the improper use of information or position for financial benefit. 6

Policy

Wagtail Services maintains financial management and accounting systems that:

  • are transparent and accountable;
  • allow for full and accurate record keeping;
  • allow budgeting and reporting;
  • meet applicable Australian Accounting Standards; and
  • support all relevant financial compliance and reporting requirements.

All financial transactions, including receipts and payments related to NDIS support delivery, are clearly identifiable and easily tracked within Wagtail Servicesâ€™ financial system.

Wagtail Services complies with the Australian Accounting Standards issued by the Australian Accounting Standards Board.

With respect to NDIS service delivery in particular, Wagtail Services:

  • implements financial processes that support Participants to avoid or limit debts to Wagtail Services;
  • manages debtors to ensure its financial sustainability; and
  • complies with the NDIS Terms of Business for Registered Providers and the NDIS Price Guide, including:
    • prohibition on advance payments for Agency Managed plans;
    • prohibition of late payment fees, and
    • processes for withdrawal or termination of services.

Procedures

General

Wagtail Servicesâ€™ Directors (and External Accountant):

  • uses the NDIS Portal to support Wagtail Servicesâ€™ financial management;
  • maintains accurate and up-to-date records of the businessâ€™ Bank Accounts; and
  • monitors Wagtail Servicesâ€™ financial position to minimise the risk of fraud and ensure expenditure complies with the budget, is accounted for correctly, and is properly authorised prior to expenditure being incurred.

All purchases must be reconciled in Wagtail Servicesâ€™ financial recording database.

The Directors (and External Accountant) are responsible for authorising and reimbursing payments for work-related expenses incurred by staff, where appropriate.

The Directors are responsible for ensuring that processes are in place and are funds available to pay for:

  • staff wages and salaries;
  • long service leave, sick leave and accumulated annual leave entitlements;
  • equipment and other assets;
  • training and development;
  • maintenance; and
  • unexpected expenses.

The External Accountant also responsible for ensuring all debts incurred by Wagtail Services are settled by their due date or for arranging extensions for payment where this is necessary.

Participants, their supporters and other stakeholders have access to Wagtail Servicesâ€™ feedback and complaints processes and can raise issues about the businessâ€™ financial management processes without fear of retribution.

Assets

Details of all assets owned by Wagtail Services are recorded in an Asset Register. When an asset is sold or otherwise disposed of, the details of the disposal (such as sale proceeds) are recorded in the Asset Register.

Where an asset is lost, damaged, or destroyed, Wagtail Services will repair or replace the asset if it is still required. Asset depreciation will be recorded in accordance with the Australian Accounting Standards.

Insurance

The Directors are responsible for ensuring all people and equipment associated with Wagtail Servicesâ€™ operations are covered by all necessary insurances. Insurances are recorded in an Insurances Register, which includes the type of insurance, policy name and number, annual premium and expiry date of each policy.

Insurance policies must be renewed no less than 14 days before their expiry and reflect the businessâ€™ requirements as well as market conditions.

Budget Processes and Financial Planning

The External Accountant prepares annual itemised budgets for Wagtail Services for each financial year in consultation with Wagtail Servicesâ€™ independent accountant. Budget development takes into account the current and previous year's income and expenditure, any known changes to funding arrangements and expected cost changes.

As part of financial planning and preparing the budget, the External Accountant will record all important assumptions, develop a break-even analysis and prepare a projected profit and loss report.

Annual budgets for each financial year must be endorsed by the Leadership Team by no later than the July of that financial year.

Payroll

Payroll is processed fortnightly by the External Accountant.

The External Accountant prepares quarterly financial reports for review by the Leadership Team.

In each new financial year, the External Accountant will ensure that the previous year's financials are documented and archived.

Quarterly Reporting

Financial reporting will also include reviewing performance against set KPIs, including:

  • the number of new clients vs target;
  • operating costs vs budget;
  • supplier costs vs budget; and
  • payroll costs vs budget.

Financial reports will be prepared and overseen by the External Accountant.

External Reporting

The External Accountant is responsible for reconciling Wagtail Servicesâ€™ accounts at the end of each financial quarter and submitting Business Activity Statements to the Australian Tax Office (ATO) within the required timeframe.

The External Accountant is also responsible for reporting Superannuation and Pay As You Go (PAYG) tax withholding amounts to the ATO. Visualcare has Single Touch Payroll (STP) functionality that allows Wagtail Services to report tax and super information for all staff to the ATO as payroll is processed.

All Superannuation must be paid to the correct super funds within the timeframe required. BAS and PAYG amounts must be paid to the ATO by their due date. The External Accountant must contact the ATO should Wagtail Services be unable to make any of these payments on time.

Fraud and Corruption

Wagtail Servicesâ€™ Directors and Manager are responsible for ensuring staff are trained in and understand what fraudulent practices are, how to identify potential fraud and how to report fraud.

Where a staff member, Participant or other stakeholder identifies suspected fraud or corruption, they must make an initial report to:

  • the Directors (unless that person may be implicated); or
  • the Police.

All reports of fraud or corruption must be treated confidentially and referred to the Leadership Team. The Directors will record all reports of actual and suspected fraud or corruption, noting the nature of the report, the time received, and remedial actions planned and taken. A copy of these records must be made available to relevant authorities upon request.

Any internal discipline or misconduct investigations relating to staff as a result of a fraud or corruption allegation must be conducted in accordance with Wagtail Servicesâ€™ Human Resources Policy and Procedure.

If, during the course of a disciplinary investigation, it is determined that a criminal offence such as fraud or corruption may have been committed, a report must be made to the police. To ensure that a criminal investigation is not compromised, clearance may need to be sought from the police for Wagtail Services to:

  • continue the disciplinary investigation;
  • notify the staff member of misconduct allegations;
  • interview witnesses to inform a disciplinary investigation; and
  • release information to the staff member or their representative.

NDIS Considerations

Wagtail Servicesâ€™ financial arrangements regarding NDIS service delivery must comply with:

  • the NDIS Act 2013 (Cth), the NDIS Rules, all relevant NDIS guidelines, and all policies issued by the NDIA, including the NDIS Terms of Business for Registered Providers; and
  • all other relevant Commonwealth or state law and other requirements.

Pricing for supports must be endorsed by the Leadership Team and be based on the price controls and quoting requirements set out in the NDIS Price Guide, as well as the NDIS Provider SIL Pack Training Guide and FAQs and related templates.

Wagtail Services must not:

  • add charges to the cost of supports, such as credit card surcharges or any additional fees;
  • request payment before the support has been delivered; or
  • charge cancellation fees, except in circumstances identified in the NDIS Price Guide.

Pricing for supports provided to Participants who self-manage their NDIS funding may be negotiated with the Participant or their representative independently to the NDIS Price Guide.

GST will not be charged on supports that are considered GST-free under the GST-free Supply (National Disability Insurance Scheme Supports) Determination 2017.

The Directors must maintain full and accurate accounts and financial records of the supports delivered to NDIS Participants, along with records of all Service Agreements. All financial records relating to NDIS service delivery must accurately detail the quantity, type and duration of support delivered.

Service Agreements

Service Agreements are not mandatory and Participants are free to decline an offer to enter into a Service Agreement with Wagtail Services. If this occurs, a member of the Leadership Team will communicate to the Participant the benefits of entering into a Service Agreement. If the Participant still declines, reasons will be sought and these will be recorded. If a Service Agreement is to be developed, staff must work collaboratively with the Participant and their supports to develop Service Agreements that promote Participant goals and choice and control over both their supports and finances.

Staff must disclose all fees and charges to Participants before providing supports and include this information in Service Agreements.

Service Agreements must clearly set out the fees to be paid for supports, when delivery of supports is to be performed and the method of payment required for those supports. See Wagtail Servicesâ€™ Assessment, Planning and Review Policy and Procedure for more information about Service Agreements and what they must contain.

Service Bookings

Service Bookings apply to Participants whose NDIS funding is managed by the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) or a registered NDIS Plan Manager. Service bookings formalise the supports to be provided to individual Participants in the NDIS myplace portal and contain key information from the Service Agreement, including the supports to be delivered, the period supports are required and the cost.

The Directors, or authorised employees, are responsible for entering and managing all Wagtail Services service bookings for NDIA-managed Participants.

Payment

Once supports have been delivered to Participants whose funds are managed by the NDIA, the Director or authorised employee is responsible for submitting payment requests through the myplace portal, against the relevant Service Bookings.

Payment requests are processed fortnightly. Payment requests must be submitted within 60 days from the end of the relevant Service Booking.

Statements are issued to all NDIA-managed Participants or their nominated person fortnightly to provide them with regular summaries of supports delivered and fees charged for those services.

Should Wagtail Services experience any issues with payment requests, they can refer to the NDIAâ€™s provider toolkit at www.ndis.gov.au or phone the Agency on 1800 800 110.

Once supports have been delivered to a Participant who is self-managing their funding, the Participant or their nominated person will be invoiced directly.

Once supports have been delivered to a Participant who engages a registered Plan Manager, the Plan Manager will be issued an invoice so that they can process it on the Participantâ€™s behalf.

Invoices are processed fortnightly. Invoice requests must be approved and submitted to an authorised employee before invoices are processed. Copies of invoices for self-managed and plan-managed Participants will be sent to the Participant or their nominated person for their information.

Record keeping

Wagtail Services must comply with the NDIAâ€™s Provider Payment Assurance Program, which confirms the accuracy of payment requests submitted by registered providers. This includes:

  • recording details of all supports provided and fees charged. At a minimum, this includes recording the Participantâ€™s name, date/s and total hours or quantity of the support delivered and the support type; and
  • maintaining records that provide evidence of the supports provided. Records that must be kept include:
    • Service Agreements containing the schedule, cost, type and quality of supports to be delivered as well as the expected outcomes for the Participant;
    • approved quotes (where relevant); and
    • evidence of the amount of support provided and the type of support provided.

The Provider Payment Assurance Program section of the NDIS Provider Toolkit (see www.providertoolkit.ndis.gov.au) provides further detail about the records that should be kept for different support types.

Financial records and accounts relating to NDIS service provision must be retained for at least 5 years from the date of issue. Wagtail Servicesâ€™ recordkeeping must also comply with all relevant legislation and requirements detailed in Schedule 1, Legislative References.

Debtor Management

Where someone has difficulty paying fees, they are encouraged to discuss this with Wagtail Services so that mutually acceptable payment arrangements can be put in place.

Outstanding debts relating to self-managed or fee-for-service supports are reviewed fortnightly by the manager, who arranges for contact with people who have fees that are 14 and 28 days overdue. The following approach will be taken at each point:

  • 14 days â€“ courtesy reminder phone call or message and follow up email or letter;
  • 28 days - courtesy reminder phone call or message and follow up email or letter. The Directors will consider terminating services based on the outcome, or devise a payment plan.

All contacts relating to outstanding debts, payments that are made and extensions or payment plan arrangements put in place as a result of following up debtors must be recorded. All agreements made with debtors or decisions made by the Directors / Manager must also be communicated in writing to the debtor after verbal contact.

Prior to referring a debt to a debt collection agency or process, the Directors / Manager must first determine whether this is a financially worthwhile option for Wagtail Services.
